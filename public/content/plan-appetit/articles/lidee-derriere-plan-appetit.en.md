# The idea behind Plan Appétit

*From an idea to an app in production.*

Even with AI, building an application from scratch doesn't happen in a month. Here is how it all started.

## From ticketing to cooking

It all began with a need from my father-in-law. He had spent 27 years in the event-ticketing world and wanted to switch to his passion: cooking. He trained and planned to open a restaurant.

As part of his retraining toward entrepreneurship, he used tools provided by France Travail (the French employment agency), but they didn't meet his needs for forecasting and building the restaurant's business plan. Concretely, he needed to figure out how to price menus based on covers, multiply that by the number of working days, account for holiday periods, and end up with a first revenue estimate he could present to banks and to France Travail.

## I choose the name Plan Appétit

So one day he called me to walk me through, in detail, the features of a tool he would need — a tool we then co-built. To name it, I chose **Plan Appétit**, a nod to *planning* the business plan. On top of that, I find it's a name that sounds good: pleasant to say, neither pretentious nor silly.

## I build the first AI recipe generator

At the time, I was between two freelance contracts, so I had the time to build the tool. And besides — if he needs it, then why not others too?

In parallel, I had been having fun automating tasks and building applications powered by generative AI, especially to make it return a well-structured, normalized output — so I could save the results into a database and turn them into usable data. That's how I built the first AI recipe generator inside Plan Appétit: ingredients, steps, a title, a description, and there you go, all saved.

## A tool both powerful and easy to use

I added more features and more interactive ways to prompt. I discovered a great hobby: imagining a system that accounts for the design serving the function, that lets you prompt intelligently, while combining algorithms and data manipulation — my area of expertise — to build a tool that is both powerful and easy to use.
